Pantone Matching System

What is Pantone?

The Pantone Matching System is a standardized system for describing colors; it is used commonly across manufacturing and design fields. As of March 2019, Pantone has named 1,867 colors with many added each year. They maintain separate color sets for CMYK printing and RGB dyeing applications and include metallic and fluorescent colors.

The system began in 1962 when Pantone Inc, which manufactured color cards for cosmetic companies, was bought by employee Lawrence Herbert. He’d puzzled over the problem of how to accurately color pantyhose, all of which were slight variations of beige, and wound up mixing the inks himself because he couldn’t communicate the colors to manufacturers.

Greenery

Greenery was selected for 2017 as a symbol of new beginnings. Pantone describes it as fresh, zesty, renewing, and evocative of the unity of the natural world.

Did you know?

Calvin Klein kept a Pantone chip in his kitchen so his staff would know what color to make his coffee.

Team Coco Orange

Pantone developed a color of orange especially for Conan O'Brien. It's meant to be energizing, fearless, warmhearted, and animated.

Creme de Peche

Creme de Peche was selected by Pantone to be part of 2019-2020 Paris fashion week. Designers look to Pantone for predictions of color trends.

Jay-Z Blue

Until recently, Jay-Z was the only individual to have their own Pantone color, Jay-Z Blue. Now one other individual, real estate tycoon Sherry Chris, has her own shade of hot pink.

Custom colors

If you have enough money, you can hire Pantone to develop a custom color for your brand. Pantone is responsible for Barbie pink, Tiffany's aqua, and every color used by the U.S. Army.
